Group Reflection

What went well

Trello was an awesome tool. It kept meetings short and allowed us to organize ourselves autonomously before, during and after tasks were completed. It was also a tool to see contribution to ensure workload was balanced evenly across the group. Most of the time, the group had little issue with communication and had shared expectations and goals. At our groups peak efficiency, the tasks were completed well and reviewed by each other.
All group members, except for one experiencing personal difficulties, had 100% attendance rate to meetings. This showed commitment to the group and made a big positive difference overall.

What could be improved

The group focused too much on the code side of the project instead of the written content. we would have benefited from doing smaller parts over a longer time instead of finishing sections all at once like we did and then reviewing once.
The group would have benefited from closer inspection of the marking rubric. We did not balance our time invested for marks gained.
Throughout the project john experienced family difficulties which he says interfered with his ability to work on the project. Although out of our control, more concise communication would have allowed us to share his workload and cause a lot less stress. Towards the end this did improve.

One thing that was surprising

Trello more than halved our previous meeting time. It was amazing just how much more efficient one tool made the group.

One thing we learn ’t about groups

Get onto everything early and produce a constant stream of work. Consistency leads to higher quality work compared to doing large batches

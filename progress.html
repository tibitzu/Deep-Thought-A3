How the idea was formulated....

Tibor started playing table-top roleplaying games over 30 years ago and video games for about 25 years. One thing that he noted was that game titles were often very linear, with a set path that the characters are on, even when they do not look linear, in fact they are. He had always dreamt of making a truly open-world, open-ended video game. As games continued to evolve and games started appearing online in a MMORPG (Massive Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Games) format, games still had a static, linear formulated which is both annoying and frustrating. Tibor envisioned a game that would allow players to explore a world together which was ever expanding, where they could make their own paths through the world and even build their own locations.

Elijah has been playing video games as long as he can remember, and has been enjoying table-top roleplaying games for 4 years now. He loves both the RPG genre and the roguelike genre so when Tibor suggested the idea, he was happy to contribute.

Me being an avid gamer as well like most of the group this seemed like an incredibly good step in a positive direction for us, I think we all like playing games sometimes too much so being able to create a game keeps us focused and still enjoying the process throughout the coding and the managerial side of things as well.

Plans for No Retreat:

The plan for the multiplayer roguelike is to start small, although the game is large in scope, our limits are also great. As a result of our little experience, the plan is the create four main things; a map of a town where the players will start and can move around, a draft combat system with which to interact with monsters, a basic character generator to make characters with stats, and a welcome screen to start the game with some simple art on it. For parts we struggle to or cannot complete we plan to investigate what skills we may need, who else we may need to complete the project, and how we may implement multiplayer functionality and how may save the map content.

The Map:

The map will be simple. A landscape console with a wall surrounding a filled area of ground tiles, some houses which will be procedurally generated and a road running through it. A character represented by an @ symbol which the player can move around by using the keyboard arrow keys. The character will not be able move through the houses or walls but can move upon the ground and road tiles.

The Combat System:

The combat system is inspired by an online browser game Candy Box 2. This system is being developed by Elijah Goodlet. The core premise is that the two combatants will relentlessly charge at each other until one fall decided by their character statistics.
